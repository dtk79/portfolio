The Work

Target

I've had a lot of fun and learned LOTS while working with Target over the years on several initiatives. Much of the most recent work is below. What you won't find are artifcats of the countless hours of interviews, user testing and heartache that went into preparing this work.

In addition to my work on Target.com I also had the opportunity to consult on a project for Target's Global Photography initiative - an effort to consolidate all digital photography work to a select few global operations centers. I worked to design the interface various users throughout the workflow would need to usher the work to the next point in the process, ultimately indexing and cataloging the assets available and allowing them to be used in digital and print forms.

Product Recommendations

I lead UX and design for all permutations of Target's first in-house product recommendations engine. The guest-facing interface presented many opportunities to offer users helpful alternatives or additions to their baskets. On the back end, another initiative took place to provide our site merchants with the tools necessary to administer these recommendations based on merchandising strategies, promotions, and other factors.

Raings & Reviews

Worked for a short time on Target.com's ratings and reviews interface. The challenge for reading reviews became one of getting the most useful information to the user as quickly as possible.

My Account > Write a Review

Writing a review became a balancing act of getting as much useful information from our users as possible, while maintining an easy and delightful interface to gather their insights for the benefit of other guests.

Product Detail Page: Q&A exploration

I began work on a new Questions and Answers section on the product detail page to house content related to user reviews. This shows an exploration into the design thinking, but ultimately I left before completion.

Virtual Bundle Shoping Process on mobile

Architected virtual bundle functionality for Target.com's desktop and mobile views, allowing users to create bundles of products for added value. Process included touch points throughout the user journey, including browse, search, bundle creation, cart, checkout and fulfillment.

Promotional framework

Expanded baseline strategy through all permutations of promotional messaging on Target.com.
